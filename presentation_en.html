What's my story with computer science?

I started learning computer science in 2017, after visiting the Pierre Mendès France middle school in Saint-André (Pyrénées-Orientales). Thanks to my father I already had the basics of IT as he is passionate by the field and self-taught.

During the visit I discovered Scratch, but I quickly wanted to learn actual languages, and naive as I was I chose to learn HTML (but I'm absolutely rusted in that language). After a few months I was getting bored of HTML and CSS (No JS at the time, as I always found the language to be awful).

After finally understanding HTML wasn't a programming language, I then learnt a bit of PHP and Ruby. But I completely lost all of my knowledge of those languages.

During summer 2018, in the midst of my parents' separation, topped off by a move in Pas-de-Calais, I found out web development wasn't made for me, thus I started learning Python (3.6 at the time), on which I really learnt the key notions of programming: logic, algorithms, functions, OOP, etc... .

At the time I was mainly making programs just for the sake of making some or to automate calculations in maths when were in a quite repetitive unit.

In the beginning of 10th grade I started to diversify my programming knowledge, and began learning Java, and quickly got bored.

At the end of the same year I started learning C, its simple syntax quickly seduced me despite the language's reputation. And 11th grade's end I started learning C#, not for developping on Unity but for software engineering, I quickly got into it even though Java, which is a close language, didn't convinced me.

Now in 12th in SIN, I put to good use my experience accumulated throughout the years to help others on their code: debugging it, improving it, explaining concepts.

I currently know 3 programming languages : Python, C and C#.
